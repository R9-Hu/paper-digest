    Look Again Before You Abstain:Budgeted Conformal Evidence Acquisition for Reliable Vision-Language Model¶

    🕒 Published (v1): 2026-06-15 13:02 UTC · Source: Arxiv · link

    TL;DR¶

    Large vision-language models (LVLMs) hallucinate, and conformal selective prediction can bound hallucination rates—but at crippling abstention cost (>80% of claims dropped to hit 5% error). BCEA replaces the binary answer/abstain decision with a three-way choice (answer, abstain, or acquire more visual evidence via zooming/cropping/intervention), and proves that naively plugging acquisition into a calibrated filter violates the statistical guarantee, while re-calibrating on post-acquisition scores restores it. The result is higher coverage at the same certified hallucination bound.

    Problem¶

    Conformal selective prediction for LVLMs (e.g., ConfLVLM) provides distribution-free hallucination-rate guarantees but abstains on the vast majority of claims—82% must be dropped to certify ≤5% hallucination on balanced existence claims. Existing evidence-acquisition methods (e.g., ReCoVERR) reduce over-abstention but use heuristic risk thresholds without any statistical guarantee, and only gather textual evidence rather than re-examining pixels. No prior method combines guaranteed hallucination control with visual evidence acquisition.

    Method¶

    BCEA operates at the level of individual atomic claims extracted from LVLM responses. It defines an evidence-sufficiency score comparing claim log-likelihood under the original image versus a targeted intervention: global ungrounding (s_glob = ℓ(x) − ℓ(∅)) for existence/attribute claims, and claim-type-specific interventions (e.g., horizontal flip for left/right spatial claims, where s_flip = ℓ(x) − ℓ(flip(x)) has predictable sign depending on truth value).

    When a claim's score is borderline (below the assert threshold, not clearly ungrounded), BCEA acquires by scoring up to B zoomed crops: s_acq = max(ℓ(x), max_b ℓ(x^(b))) − ℓ(∅). The critical insight is that naively applying the old threshold to this inflated score breaks conformal exchangeability—realized risk overshoots by up to 17 points (Proposition 1). The fix: treat the entire acquisition policy as part of the scoring function and re-calibrate the Clopper-Pearson threshold on post-acquisition scores from the calibration set. Theorem 1 proves this restores the finite-sample selective risk guarantee at level α. CLIP similarity to "a photo of ⟨object⟩" guides crop selection without ground-truth localization. Theorem 2 characterizes exactly when acquisition helps: iff the post-acquisition ROC dominates the pre-acquisition ROC pointwise.

    Key Contributions¶

    • Formal demonstration that naive acquisition (reuse old threshold on inflated scores) silently violates conformal guarantees, with empirical overshoot up to 17 percentage points above target risk.
    • Theorem 1: folding the acquisition policy into the score and re-calibrating on post-acquisition scores restores the finite-sample hallucination-rate guarantee.
    • Proposition 3: sequential, within-claim adaptive acquisition (look, judge, look again, stop) is valid without correction because stopping never consults calibration labels.
    • Theorem 2: a clean ROC-dominance characterization of when acquisition helps—converts a vague design question into a falsifiable, measurable criterion.
    • Structured claim-type-specific interventions: horizontal flip lifts spatial left/right verification from 0.574 to 0.765 AUROC, where global ungrounding is at chance.
    • CLIP-guided crop selection (no ground-truth location): nearly doubles acquisition gain on small objects vs. uniform grid.
    • Anytime-valid risk control via betting confidence sequences for optional-stopping calibration streams (Proposition 4).

    Results¶

    • Existence claims, LLaVA-1.5 (1,440 claims): At α=0.10, No-Acq asserts 28% of claims; BCEA asserts 37% at the same certified risk. At α=0.20, No-Acq 39% vs. BCEA 47%. 90th-percentile realized risk tracks target (0.06/0.10/0.21 vs. targets 0.05/0.10/0.20).
    • Naive acquisition violates guarantee: At α=0.20, naive method achieves 74% coverage but 90th-percentile risk 0.41 (target 0.20)—2× violation.
    • Spatial relations, LLaVA-1.5 (996 claims): Global score AUROC 0.574 (near chance); horizontal flip intervention AUROC 0.765.
    • Acquisition budget scaling: Guaranteed coverage rises monotonically from 0.29 (B=0) to 0.35 (B=5) at α=0.10 while realized risk stays pinned.
    • CLIP vs. uniform grid vs. no acquisition (existence, LLaVA-1.5): AUROC 0.824 → 0.862 → 0.882; coverage at α=0.10: 0.28 → 0.33 → 0.37.
    • POPE benchmark, 4 VLMs × 3 splits (~11.5k claims): Acquisition improves AUROC in all 12 settings (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT random: 0.56 → 0.72); BCEA beats guaranteed abstention in coverage at every setting; 90th-percentile risk within ~0.03 of α=0.10 throughout.
    • Qwen2.5-VL-7B: At α=0.05, abstention asserts 1% of claims; BCEA asserts 15% at same certified risk. Global score at chance for spatial relations (AUROC 0.50).
    • Budget allocation: Greedy vs. uniform budget allocation statistically indistinguishable (≤0.4 coverage points), as predicted by theory.

    Limitations¶

    • Only existence and left/right spatial claims work; counting, relative size, above/below, color, and color-object binding all have near-chance AUROC (0.50–0.58) with no effective intervention found, yielding zero coverage.
    • Vertical flip for above/below fails (AUROC 0.50) because the flipped image is out-of-distribution; valid interventions require in-distribution truth-flipping transforms, which must be designed per claim type.
    • Evaluated only on probed atomic claims (COCO-constructed and POPE yes/no); not yet validated on self-generated claims from free-form model output, where claim parsing noise could interact with conformal calibration.
    • Method scope is bounded by backbone perception: where the VLM cannot reliably perceive a property, evidence acquisition cannot recover signal that the model never had.
    • Tested on 7–8B parameter models; behavior at larger scales unknown.
    • Per-claim budget allocation provides no coverage benefit in the current existence-claim setup (structural reason identified, but this limits fine-grained resource management).

    Relevance to Vision-Language Models¶

    BCEA directly addresses LVLM hallucination—one of the central reliability bottlenecks for deploying VLMs in high-stakes settings—by providing the first method that combines distribution-free hallucination-rate guarantees with active visual evidence acquisition. It reveals a subtle but empirically severe failure mode: any conformal VLM filter that acquires evidence without re-calibrating on post-acquisition scores silently loses its guarantee, a pitfall directly relevant to anyone building reliable VLM pipelines. The structured-intervention finding (global ungrounding is blind to relational claims; claim-type-specific pixel interventions recover the signal) opens a systematic design question for VLM verification: what transformations flip claim truth value while remaining in-distribution? This connects to broader work on compositional grounding, spatial reasoning evaluation, and uncertainty quantification in VLMs.
