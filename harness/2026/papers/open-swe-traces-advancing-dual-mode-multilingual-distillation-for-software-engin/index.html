    Open-SWE-Traces: Advancing Dual-Mode Multilingual Distillation for Software Engineering Agents¶

    🕒 Published (v1): 2026-06-14 22:10 UTC · Source: Arxiv · link

    TL;DR¶

    OPEN-SWE-TRACES is a 207,489-trajectory corpus spanning nine programming languages, synthesized by running two state-of-the-art LLMs through OpenHands and SWE-agent harnesses on real-world PRs from SWE-rebench-v2. The dataset uniquely pairs "thinking" (CoT) and "non-thinking" trajectories to support dual-mode distillation. Fine-tuning Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B on this corpus yields 61.7% on SWE-bench Verified and 57.1% on SWE-bench Multilingual.

    Problem¶

    Large-scale, diverse trajectory data for training SWE agents is severely scarce, especially for non-Python languages. Evaluation benchmarks (Multi-SWEbench, SWE-PolyBench) have outpaced training resources: no prior dataset provided multilingual, harness-diverse, dual-mode (thinking/non-thinking) interaction traces at the scale needed for robust distillation.

    Method¶

    The pipeline starts from SWE-rebench-v2's 32,000+ containerized multilingual tasks (permissive-license subset: MIT, Apache, BSD). Two LLMs serve as agent backends: MiniMax-M2.5 (thinking/CoT traces) and Qwen3.5-122B-A10B (non-thinking, tool-use-diverse traces), each deployed inside both OpenHands and SWE-agent scaffolds. Raw logs undergo two-stage filtering: (1) execution aggregation discards corrupted/incomplete trajectories; (2) behavioral pruning removes task-incomplete, structurally invalid (empty patch, test-suite tampering), and malformed tool-use trajectories. A post-hoc TrajectoryScanner uses AST parsing to detect and remove "git hacking" — commands like git reflog or git blame that leak solution metadata. The resulting 207,489 trajectories (65,244 resolved, ~40.6% pass rate) are stored in a unified schema preserving reasoning_content. Distillation fine-tunes the Qwen3-30B-A3B series with lr=1e-5, batch=32, context=131,072 tokens; the full dual-mode model uses the /think / /no_think trigger at inference time.

    Key Contributions¶

    • OPEN-SWE-TRACES dataset: 207,489 trajectories across Python, Go, TypeScript, JavaScript, Rust, Java, PHP, C, C++; largest multilingual SWE trajectory corpus to date.
    • Dual-mode corpus structure: deliberate pairing of CoT (MiniMax-M2.5) and non-CoT (Qwen3.5-122B) traces enabling switchable thinking/non-thinking inference.
    • TrajectoryScanner: AST-based integrity validator that detects and removes git-hacking behaviors at the Bash-command level.
    • OPEN-SWE-AGENT: open-source Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B fine-tune achieving 61.7% SWE-bench Verified, 57.1% SWE-bench Multilingual, 36.8% SWE-bench Pro.
    • Ablation study: isolates contributions of harness choice, Python-only vs. multilingual data, and resolved-only vs. full-corpus filtering.

    Results¶

    • SWE-bench Verified: OPEN-SWE-AGENT (/no_think, MSWEA) achieves 61.7%; (/think, MSWEA) achieves 59.3%. Baseline Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B (no fine-tune): 51.6%. Best comparable open MoE 30B-A3B: Scale-SWE-Agent 64.0% (trained on ~71k Python-only trajectories vs. 24k PRs here).
    • SWE-bench Multilingual: OPEN-SWE-AGENT (/no_think, MSWEA) achieves 57.1% vs. base model 33.5% (+23.6 pp); Scale-SWE-Agent 45.2%.
    • SWE-bench Pro: OPEN-SWE-AGENT (/no_think) achieves 36.8% vs. base 28.4%.
    • CoT compression: thinking traces reduce average assistant turns from 94.08 → 58.22 per trajectory in OpenHands (−38%).
    • Cross-lingual transfer ablation: multilingual → Python-only data drops SWE-M no-think from 57.1% → 43.1% (−14 pp).
    • Data filtering ablation: resolved-only vs. full corpus: SWE-M think 40.5% → 47.6%; no-think 49.6% → 57.1%.
    • Cross-harness penalty: MOH-trained → MSWEA transfer incurs steeper degradation than MSWEA → MOH.

    Limitations¶

    • MOpenHands (MOH) harness causes looping in 5–10% of OPEN-SWE-AGENT runs, exhausting max turn limits; MOH excluded from SWE-bench Pro evaluation.
    • The thinking-mode variant consistently underperforms the non-thinking variant on multilingual benchmarks, suggesting insufficient training volume to develop robust internal reasoning for non-Python languages.
    • OPEN-SWE-AGENT-INSTRUCT lags Scale-SWE-Agent by ~2.3 pp on SWE-bench Verified, attributed partly to smaller training volume (24k vs. ~71k trajectories).
    • C and C++ are severely underrepresented (683 and 302 trajectories respectively), limiting coverage.
    • Cross-harness generalization is structurally constrained: models overfit to the interaction patterns of their training harness.
    • No SWE-bench Pro results for MOpenHands due to persistent integration failures.

    Relevance to Harnesses / Meta-Harnesses¶

    This work is directly relevant to harness research because it treats the agent harness (OpenHands vs. SWE-agent) as a first-class experimental variable rather than an interchangeable scaffold: the ablation on cross-harness generalization demonstrates that harness-specific interaction patterns, action spaces, and observation formats cause measurable performance degradation when a model trained on one harness is evaluated on another. The TrajectoryScanner constitutes a harness-level integrity meta-layer — AST-parsed validation applied across harness outputs to enforce behavioral correctness invariants. The dual-harness synthesis strategy (running both OpenHands and SWE-agent to generate diverse trajectories) is itself a meta-harness pattern, combining heterogeneous scaffolds to produce a richer, less biased training distribution than any single harness can provide.
