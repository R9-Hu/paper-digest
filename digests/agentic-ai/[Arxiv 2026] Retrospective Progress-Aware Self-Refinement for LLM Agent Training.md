# Retrospective Progress-Aware Self-Refinement for LLM Agent Training

## TL;DR
RePro (Retrospective Progress-Aware Training) trains LLM agents to self-generate metacognitive progress signals by first executing a task online, then retrospectively reassessing step-wise completion percentages given the known outcome. A pilot study shows online progress prompting degrades performance (−8.6% avg) while retrospective demonstrations help (+7.9%), motivating a dedicated two-phase training framework. RePro achieves up to +11.57% absolute success rate gains over baseline RL on WebShop, ALFWorld, and Sokoban.

## Problem
RL-trained LLM agents optimize step-level action selection but lack metacognitive awareness of long-horizon task progress—they cannot reliably assess what has been accomplished, what remains, or whether the current trajectory is on track. Sparse terminal rewards make this worse: agents receive no intermediate signal about trajectory-level standing. Naive online progress prompting is unreliable and actively harmful because the agent must predict completion probability under uncertainty, injecting noise into decision-making.

## Method
RePro is a two-phase framework built on a **forward-then-reflect** rollout paradigm:

1. **Forward Execution**: at each step t, the agent generates an online progress estimate p̃t ∈ [0,100] alongside action at, conditioned on interaction history ht.
2. **Retrospective Reflection**: after trajectory completion with known outcome r, the agent re-assesses step-wise progress (p1,...,pT) conditioned on the full trajectory τ and r. Successful trajectories anchor pT = 1 (100%), providing a supervisory signal.
3. **Retrospection Warmup (SFT)**: a small dataset Dwarmup of retrospectively-labeled trajectories generated by an external model (DeepSeek-V4) teaches the agent the forward-then-reflect format via supervised fine-tuning on both action and retrospective progress prediction.
4. **RePro-PO (RL)**: policy optimization with a composite per-step reward:
   - **renv**: sparse environment outcome reward
   - **rp(t) = β·(pt+1 − pt)**: progress shaping via retrospective progress deltas
   - **ralign(t) = α·max(0, 1−|pt−p̃t|)/T**: online-retrospective alignment reward pushing online estimates toward retrospective quality
   - **rformat**: format compliance + boundary penalties (p0≠0 or pT≠1 on success)
   
   Hierarchical advantage estimation combines episode-level normalization (across trajectories from the same task) with step-level normalization (across actions from identical anchor observations, following GiGPO), using a PPO-clipped objective.

## Key Contributions
- **Pilot study** revealing a clear asymmetry: online progress prompting uniformly hurts (−8.6% avg), while retrospective demonstrations help (+7.9% avg), demonstrating that progress awareness requires dedicated training, not just prompting
- **RePro framework**: forward-then-reflect paradigm with Retrospection Warmup + RePro-PO composite reward, enabling self-generated progress metacognition without a separate reward model
- **Empirical validation** across WebShop, ALFWorld, Sokoban on Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B/7B and Qwen3-4B with progress-quality analysis showing RePro agents develop genuine outcome-discriminating progress estimates

## Results
- **WebShop** (vs. Meta Prompt baseline):
  - Qwen2.5-1.5B: +8.98% SR (Best: 81.64%), +2.29 Score
  - Qwen2.5-3B: +11.57% SR (Best: 83.59%), +6.37 Score
  - Qwen2.5-7B: +5.82% SR (Best: 84.38%), +4.04 Score
- **ALFWorld**:
  - Qwen2.5-1.5B: +11.72 SR (Best: 99.22%), +3.78 Score
  - Qwen2.5-7B: +4.69 SR (Best: 100.00%), +2.29 Score
- **Sokoban** (Qwen3-4B): +3.12 SR (Best: 88.28%), +0.67 Score
- **Progress quality** (Intermediate Discrimination vs. Meta Prompt baseline):
  - Baseline: −1.07 to +1.86 across model sizes
  - RePro: 6.37 (1.5B), 31.62 (3B), 11.14 (7B)
- Retrospective progress deltas ∆p positively correlate with step-level advantages in 91.5% of training rollouts
- Outperforms all ablation variants (Warmup Only, Warmup+GiGPO, w/o Warmup, L1/L2 reward variants)

## Limitations
- Progress awareness is represented solely as verbalized numeric estimates (0–100%); latent-state or hidden-vector representations are unexplored
- Evaluation restricted to WebShop, ALFWorld, and Sokoban; not tested on GUI automation, open-ended embodied interaction, or real-world agentic pipelines
- No ground-truth progress labels exist; retrospective signals are derived from trajectory outcomes, not human annotations or verified intermediate states
- External LLM (DeepSeek-V4) required for generating Retrospection Warmup demonstrations—a dependency that may not scale or may introduce distributional bias

## Relevance to Agentic AI / LLM Agents
RePro directly addresses a fundamental bottleneck in long-horizon agent RL: the credit assignment problem under sparse terminal rewards, tackled here via self-generated metacognitive signals rather than external process reward models. The finding that retrospective (post-hoc) self-assessment enables what online (predictive) self-assessment cannot is a sharp empirical insight with broad implications for agent training pipelines. The framework complements existing policy optimization methods (GRPO, GiGPO) as a drop-in auxiliary supervision layer, making it applicable to any outcome-rewarded agentic RL setting. The asymmetry between prompting and training for eliciting meta-awareness is a practically important negative result for the growing literature on inference-time reflection.
