# Beyond English: Uncovering the Multilingual Gap in Vision-Language-Action Models

*🕒 **Published (v1):** 2026-06-14 10:04 UTC  ·  **Source:** Arxiv  ·  [link](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15714v1)*

## TL;DR
Current Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models exhibit severe performance degradation on non-English instructions despite being built on multilingual VLM backbones, because the language-to-action alignment process biases models toward English. The authors provide the first systematic multilingual evaluation framework for VLAs, identify representation shift as the root cause, and propose Multilingual Principal Component Alignment (MPCA) to close the gap.

## Problem
VLA models are trained and benchmarked almost exclusively on English instructions. Although their underlying VLM backbones (e.g., Qwen-VL) possess multilingual representations, it is unknown whether this multilinguality survives the action-alignment fine-tuning process. No prior benchmark quantifies multilingual instruction-following degradation in embodied agents.

## Method
**Dataset construction:** English instructions from LIBERO and SimplerEnv are extended via Cloud Translation API into Chinese, French, Russian, and Arabic; code-switching variants are generated by LLM-based NER that replaces key verbs/nouns with their English equivalents.

**Evaluation:** A drop-in multilingual evaluation adapter replaces instructions with one line of code; relative performance = (Success Rate_multilingual − Success Rate_English) × 100%.

**Analysis:** Middle-layer average-pooled embeddings are visualized via PCA to diagnose representation shift across languages.

**MPCA (Multilingual Principal Component Alignment):** During M-CT co-training (VLA + multilingual COCO-VQA), PCA is computed over cross-lingual embedding groups every k steps to extract a principal subspace U. A cosine-similarity alignment loss encourages non-English projected embeddings (U^T h_{i,j}) to match their English counterparts (U^T h_{i,en}), added to the standard VLM + VLA losses with weight λ.

## Key Contributions
- First systematic multilingual evaluation framework for VLA models, covering 5 languages, 2 interaction settings (multilingual + code-switching), and 2 simulation benchmarks.
- Empirical demonstration of a significant multilingual gap across π0.5, OpenVLA-OFT, ABot-M0, Cosmos Policy, and multiple Qwen-VL-based VLAs.
- Four key findings: (1) base VLM language coverage drives the gap; (2) visually homogeneous tasks amplify it; (3) English keyword retention in code-switching reduces it; (4) diffusion-based action heads (GR00T-style, π-style) are more robust than tokenizer-based heads (FAST-style).
- Representation-level diagnosis showing cross-lingual embedding distance correlates with task performance drop.
- MPCA fine-tuning method that outperforms English-only fine-tuning (E-CT), multilingual fine-tuning (M-FT), and multilingual co-training (M-CT) baselines.

## Results
- English-centric models (PaliGemma/Prismatic backbone): non-English performance approaches zero-instruction baseline (e.g., OpenVLA-OFT avg. relative drops of −27.5% to −29.8% on LIBERO; π0.5 −38% to −43.5%).
- Qwen-VL-based models show better Chinese generalization but still degrade severely on French/Russian/Arabic (Qwen3-VL GR00T avg. −33% to −48% for ru/ar on LIBERO).
- Code-switching consistently reduces the gap vs. full translation across all models and environments.
- MPCA vs. baselines on LIBERO (avg. absolute success rate): GR00T baseline ~89.1 zh / 62.3 fr / 56.5 ru / 59.3 ar; M-CT ~88.3 / 87.4 / 83.1 / 89.4; MPCA ~90.6 / 89.5 / 85.5 / 90.8 — MPCA achieves the best or second-best across all non-English languages while preserving English performance (~95.3).

## Limitations
- Evaluation is simulation-only (LIBERO, SimplerEnv); real-robot multilingual generalization is untested.
- Only four non-English languages evaluated; coverage of truly low-resource or non-Latin-script languages beyond Arabic is limited.
- Multilingual co-training (M-FT, M-CT) degrades English and Chinese performance relative to the monolingual baseline — MPCA partially but not fully resolves this trade-off.
- MPCA requires periodic PCA recomputation during training and a carefully tuned λ; computational overhead and sensitivity are not deeply analyzed.
- The COCO-VQA multilingual augmentation dataset is domain-mismatched from robotics, potentially limiting generalization to richer manipulation scenarios.

## Relevance to Vision-Language Models
This paper directly probes a failure mode at the VLM→VLA transfer boundary: multilingual representations in the backbone VLM do not automatically transfer to downstream action policies, revealing that language-action alignment is not language-neutral. For VLM researchers, this highlights that fine-tuning on downstream tasks can erode cross-lingual alignment even when the pretrained model is strongly multilingual. The MPCA technique — aligning representation subspaces rather than raw embeddings — is a lightweight, architecture-agnostic approach that could generalize to other VLM fine-tuning regimes where cross-lingual consistency matters. The finding that action head architecture (diffusion transformer vs. tokenizer-based) significantly modulates multilingual robustness adds a new design axis to VLA/VLM architectural choices.
