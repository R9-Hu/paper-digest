    🆕 Today's Digest — Vision-Language Models¶

    New papers/posts collected 2026-06-18.

    30 new today

    📋 In brief¶

    This batch is dominated by Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for robot manipulation, with the central tension being how to scale robot learning without drowning in expensive annotated trajectories — papers like ACE-Ego-0, Motion-Focused Latent Action, and μ₀ all converge on the same answer: mine unlabeled egocentric human video, extract latent action representations, and transfer to robot control with minimal downstream data. A second strong thread is the data and representation gap between VLM pretraining and physical action: AffordanceVLA, APT (Action Expert Pretraining), World Pilot, and the position paper each propose different intermediate layers — affordance forecasting, staged action-expert initialization, world-model priors, or explicit grounding interfaces — to prevent the VLM backbone from being corrupted by or disconnected from the action head. Evaluation and benchmarking emerge as a third theme, with Act2Answer, ERQA-Plus, SC3-Eval, and HOI-Edit collectively exposing blind spots that aggregate task-success metrics miss: VLAs quietly lose semantic world knowledge after fine-tuning, state-of-the-art models collapse on temporal and path-planning reasoning, and closed-loop video models can now score policies at scale with high correlation to real-world success. Cutting across all three threads is a shared diagnosis — articulated most directly in the position paper — that the field is bottlenecked not by model capacity but by the absence of principled grounding mechanisms that convert physical data into robot-usable supervision.

    📝 Today's papers¶

    Scalable Training of Spatially Grounded 2D Vision-Language Models for Radiology MICCAI 2026¶

    RadGrounder is a PaliGemma 2-based multi-task VLM for radiology that jointly performs report generation, VQA, and spatial grounding on 2D CT/MRI slices, trained without any manual spatial annotations. It is supported by RefRad2D, a new 1.2M-pair bilingual (German/English) clinical dataset with automatically derived bounding-box and segmentation labels. Token-based bounding-box detection achieves strong grounding without degrading language task performance.

    📌 Why selected: MICCAI 2026; 1.2M bilingual CT/MR dataset; spatial grounding in radiology VLMs without manual annotation

    Timage: A Generative Text-in-Image Paradigm for Fine-Tuning Vision-Language Models ECCV¶

    Timage addresses the spatial misalignment between text queries and image content in MLLMs by rendering the natural-language query as a typeset overlay directly onto the image, using a Constrained Schrödinger Bridge (cSB) generative process to determine optimal placement and appearance. This input-level intervention is training-free, plug-and-play, and backbone-agnostic. On VMCBench, a 7B backbone with Timage achieves 87.7% average accuracy, surpassing GPT-4o, Qwen2-VL-72B, and full fine-tuning baselines.

    📌 Why selected: ECCV; text-in-image geometric anchoring directly addresses fine-grained spatial reasoning gap in MLLMs

    Spectral Query-Key Product Weight Steering for Training-Free VLM Hallucination Mitigation¶

    QK Product Steering is a data-free, training-free, zero-inference-cost weight edit for reducing object hallucination in VLMs. It suppresses the dominant singular modes of the per-head query-key product matrix \(M_h = W_{q,h}^\top W_{k,g}\) in middle layers, then recovers an edited query weight via a closed-form update that keeps shared key weights fixed (GQA-safe). Across three GQA-based VLMs, the method achieves an average relative CHAIRs reduction of 4.0% with negligible capability degradation.

    📌 Why selected: Training-free, zero-cost weight steering eliminates VLM hallucination of visually absent objects

    The Hidden Evolution of Disguised Visual Context inside the VLM¶

    This paper conducts a controlled mechanistic comparison of in-context injection (IN-CT) versus layer-wise injection (LW-GC, LW-AT) VLM architectures under identical training conditions across single-image, multi-image, and video benchmarks. It reveals that visual tokens enter the LLM as "disguised" foreign signals and evolve very differently depending on the integration paradigm, with IN-CT producing smooth, high-frequency-enriched representations while layer-wise methods produce semantically discontinuous ones. These representational differences—not attention allocation—explain the consistent performance gap.

    📌 Why selected: Mechanistic study of visual token evolution inside LLM layers; multi-institution authors including Awais

    Confidence Calibration for Multimodal LLMs: An Empirical Study through Medical VQA MICCAI 2025¶

    MLLMs deployed for medical Visual Question Answering (VQA) are systematically overconfident, with a large gap between verbalized confidence and actual accuracy. This paper proposes MS-FBI (Multi-Strategy Fusion-Based Interrogation) combined with an auxiliary expert LLM to recalibrate confidence post-hoc. The method reduces Expected Calibration Error (ECE) by an average of 40% across three medical VQA benchmarks and four MLLM backbones.

    📌 Why selected: MICCAI 2025; confidence calibration mismatch in medical MLLMs creates direct misdiagnosis risk

    SPOT-E: Test-Time Entropy Shaping with Visual Spotlights for Frozen VLMs¶

    VLMs often fail on evidence-intensive tasks (chart reading, document parsing) because small, localized decisive cues are overlooked even when high-level reasoning is correct. SPOT-E is a plug-and-play test-time method that freezes the VLM backbone, optimizes a lightweight question-conditioned visual spotlight via per-instance GRPO, and uses an entropy-shaping reward—combining answer-entropy reduction with a low-entropy anchor-preservation penalty—to ensure spotlights reveal rather than erase evidence. It yields consistent gains across 9 VLM families and multiple benchmarks without any retraining.

    📌 Why selected: Test-time entropy shaping with visual spotlights adapts frozen VLMs for evidence-intensive tasks

    S-Agent: Spatial Tool-Use Elicits Reasoning for Spatial Intelligence¶

    S-Agent is a spatial tool-use agentic paradigm that reformulates VLM spatial reasoning as iterative spatio-temporal evidence accumulation rather than single-pass frame-level prediction. A VLM semantic planner orchestrates a three-level hierarchy of spatial tools (2D grounding → 3D lifting → spatial aggregation) alongside a dual-memory system, improving both training-free VLMs and a distilled 8B compact agent.

    📌 Why selected: Tool-augmented agent enables continuous 3D spatial reasoning beyond static stateless VLM inference

    NEST: Narrative Event Structures in Time for Long Video Understanding¶

    NEST is a benchmark of 1,005 full-length movies (avg. ~98 min) annotated with ~102 multimodal narrative events per film, covering temporal ordering, hierarchical composition, and long-range causal dependencies. It exposes a sharp capability gap in current VLMs: grounded event discovery (ETD <8%, EL <6%, EAE <11%) remains largely unsolved, while reasoning over a given event graph (ERE up to 44.42% F1 after fine-tuning) is more tractable. The dataset is unique in requiring models to connect events separated by an average of 32 minutes of video.

    📌 Why selected: Narrative event structure benchmark exposes long video understanding gap beyond mere token-length scaling

    Occ-VLM: Occupancy Grounded Vision Language Model for Indoor Scene Understanding¶

    Occ-VLM is a 3D vision-language model for indoor scene understanding that operates purely on posed RGB images using a single 2D vision encoder. It reconstructs 3D semantic occupancy as an auxiliary geometric prior via a lightweight Occ adapter, then samples occupancy-guided foreground tokens fed into an LLM for spatial reasoning. It achieves state-of-the-art RGB-based occupancy prediction and competitive 3D VQA performance against models requiring explicit 3D inputs.

    📌 Why selected: Occupancy-grounded VLM bridges 3D scene understanding for embodied intelligence applications

    Bridging Creative Intent and Visual Quality: Creator-Driven Recurrent Video Generation with Agentic Feedback Loops ICML 2026¶

    CHIEF is a human-AI co-creation framework for iterative video generation that replaces autonomous closed-loop refinement with creator-gated iteration supported by persona-conditioned multimodal LLM feedback agents. It addresses the collapse of narrative coherence and creative direction in AI-generated long-form video by placing the creator at the center of the refinement loop. Evaluated via a student-produced 10-minute short film, it achieves a live-audience rating of 4.1/5 vs. 2.4/5 for the unrefined baseline.

    📌 Why selected: ICML 2026 acceptance; agentic feedback loop for coherent long-form video generation

    From Bounding Boxes to Visual Reasoning: An On-Policy Data Annotation Tool for Vision-Language Models¶

    ScreenAnnotator is an open-source annotation tool that closes the infrastructure gap between conventional bounding-box labelers and the data demands of grounded VLM training. It introduces a unified annotation atom schema, an on-policy human-model co-evolution loop with Bayesian quality control, and a template-driven synthesis pipeline that converts one labeling effort into multi-task VLM training data. Fine-tuning on the synthesized data yields a 35.1-point absolute accuracy gain over the base VLM on flowchart QA.

    📌 Why selected: On-policy annotation tool unifying spatial grounding and open-vocab visual reasoning

    APT: Atomic Physical Transitions for Causal Video-Language Understanding¶

    Current VLMs recognize clip-level event labels ("bounce", "collision") but fail to recover the ordered causal sequence of physical state changes that constitute those events. This paper introduces Atomic Physical Transitions (APTs)—minimal, timestamped, mechanism-typed state changes—as an intermediate causal representation, constructs a 27,303-instance mixed-source dataset, and proposes APT-Tune, a parameter-efficient fine-tuning recipe that dramatically improves transition-level recall without causing event-level forgetting.

    📌 Why selected: Atomic causal state decomposition for physical event understanding in video-language

    Does VLA Even Know the Basics? Measuring Commonsense and World Knowledge Retention in Vision-Language-Action Models¶

    VLA models—obtained by fine-tuning VLMs on robotics data—are routinely assumed to preserve their underlying world knowledge, but this assumption has never been systematically tested. Act2Answer converts VLM knowledge benchmarks into tabletop object-placement episodes so agents answer via action rather than text, isolating knowledge from control difficulty. A study of 7 VLAs and 9 VLM baselines finds that VLAs retain simple perceptual knowledge but collapse near chance on richer semantic categories, with VQA co-training as the strongest predictor of retention.

    📌 Why selected: First systematic study of commonsense knowledge retention after VLM→VLA fine-tuning

    SC3-Eval: Evaluating Robot Foundation Models via Self-Consistent Video Generation¶

    SC3-Eval is a recipe for converting a pre-trained unified video dynamics model into a scalable closed-loop evaluator for robot manipulation policies by enforcing three complementary self-consistency objectives during training and inference. Applied to seven vision-language-action (VLA) policy checkpoints, it achieves a closed-loop Pearson correlation of 0.929 and MMRV of 0.119, outperforming all three video-model baselines. Beyond aggregate success rates, it reproduces per-trajectory failure modes, enabling fine-grained policy diagnostics.

    📌 Why selected: Allen Ren + Lucy Shi; scalable video-based evaluation replacing expensive real-world rollouts

    Motion-Focused Latent Action Enables Cross-Embodiment VLA Training from Human EgoVideos¶

    This paper proposes a three-stage framework for training Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models using unlabeled egocentric human videos, eliminating the need for expensive annotated robot datasets. A Hybrid Disentangled VQ-VAE extracts motion-focused latent action codes by separating manipulation dynamics from background noise via physical masks, which then supervise VLM pre-training. With only ~50 downstream robot trajectories, the method matches or exceeds state-of-the-art VLAs trained on large annotated corpora.

    📌 Why selected: Cross-embodiment VLA pretraining from abundant human egocentric videos; data-efficiency angle

    Taming I2V models for Image HOI Editing: A Cognitive Benchmark and Agentic Self-Correcting Framework¶

    This paper introduces HOI-Edit, the first benchmark for evaluating Human-Object Interaction (HOI) image editing across three hierarchical cognitive levels, paired with HOI-Eval, a grounded VLM-as-judge metric that anchors evaluation to bounding-box-localized subject-object pairs. It also proposes SCPE (Self-Correcting Process Editing), an agentic framework that repurposes Image-to-Video (I2V) models for HOI editing by iteratively refining prompts using a dynamic failure-pattern playbook, outperforming open-source SOTA and matching or exceeding commercial models on interaction metrics.

    📌 Why selected: HOI editing benchmark exposing gap in current image editing VLMs

    Language-Instructed Vision Embeddings for Controllable and Generalizable Perception ICLR 2026¶

    LIVE (Language-Instructed Vision Embeddings) trains a vision encoder to produce dynamically conditioned embeddings \(z^{(I)} = E_{live}(x, T(q))\) at inference time, guided by a textual instruction \(q\), eliminating the need for a large LLM decoder. Trained on ~16.4M synthetic image-instruction-answer triplets distilled from Gemini 2.0 Flash, LIVE achieves +34 points on MMVP and outperforms LLaVa on GQA with fewer than 10% of its parameters.

    📌 Why selected: ICLR 2026; novel paradigm using language to steer vision embeddings for controllable perception

    Qwen-RobotManip Technical Report: Alignment Unlocks Scale for Robotic Manipulation Foundation Models¶

    Qwen-RobotManip is a Vision-Language-Action (VLA) foundation model built on Qwen-VL that addresses the core bottleneck in robotic manipulation scaling: cross-embodiment data heterogeneity prevents multi-source training from generalizing. By introducing unified alignment across representation, motion, and behavior dimensions, the model absorbs a ~38,100-hour open-source corpus and achieves emergent OOD generalization including zero-shot instruction following and cross-embodiment transfer.

    📌 Why selected: Qwen team (Alibaba); applies VLM scaling recipe to robotics manipulation

    Trust the Right Teacher: Quality-Aware Self-Distillation for GUI Grounding¶

    Naive on-policy self-distillation (OPSD) for GUI coordinate grounding degrades because student-generated prefixes can already encode wrong spatial hypotheses before the teacher conditions on them, producing unreliable coordinate-token signals. This paper introduces quality-aware self-distillation, combining soft correctness-aware gating (does the teacher's top-1 coordinate token still permit completion into the ground-truth box?) with teacher-probability scaling to calibrate per-token distillation weight. On six GUI grounding benchmarks with Qwen3.5-9B, the method achieves 72.23 macro-average accuracy, outperforming the strongest self-distillation baseline by 2.16 points.

    📌 Why selected: Quality-aware self-distillation for GUI grounding; addresses VLM agent precision gap

    Domain Generalizable Adaptation of 3D Vision-Language Models via Regularized Fine-Tuning TMLR¶

    ReFine3D is a regularized fine-tuning framework for 3D vision-language models (specifically ULIP/ULIP-2) that addresses domain generalization under limited labeled data. It combines layer-selective fine-tuning with augmentation consistency regularization, LLM-generated text diversity, point-rendered vision supervision, and test-time augmentation. On standard 3D domain generalization benchmarks it consistently outperforms the prior SOTA (PointPRC) across base-to-new, cross-dataset, corruption, and few-shot settings.

    📌 Why selected: TMLR; regularized fine-tuning for domain-generalizable 3D vision-language model adaptation

    GeneralVLA-2: Geometry-Aware Reconstruction and Governed Memory for Robot Planning¶

    GeneralVLA-2 extends the GeneralVLA hierarchical VLA framework with two planner-facing improvements: GeoFuse-MV3D, a conservative multi-view 3D reconstruction branch that fuses geometry-prior guidance with mask-verified soft visual-hull support, and a governed KnowledgeBank that replaces append-only semantic memory with explicit quality, confidence, lifecycle, and conflict metadata for precision-oriented retrieval. Together these strengthen the two evidence streams—object geometry and manipulation experience—fed to the 3DAgent planner. Evaluations on GSO-30, Terminal-Bench 2.0, SWE-Bench Verified, RLBench simulation, and real-world manipulation all show consistent improvements over respective baselines.

    📌 Why selected: Geometry-aware 3D reconstruction + memory for generalist VLA robot planning

    ERQA-Plus: A Diagnostic Benchmark for Reasoning in Embodied AI¶

    ERQA-Plus is a 1,766-question diagnostic benchmark grounded in 711 robot-centric images that evaluates fine-grained embodied reasoning across five taxonomic categories. It uses a three-agent pipeline (Generator, Judge, Reviser) to produce visually grounded, shortcut-resistant multiple-choice and open-ended QA pairs. Benchmarking reveals that even the strongest model (Qwen3-VL-32B) fails on temporal and path-planning reasoning, while domain-specialized RoboBrain2.5-8B dramatically outperforms larger general VLMs on those hard subtypes.

    📌 Why selected: Diagnostic benchmark for spatial/procedural reasoning in embodied AI agents

    ACE-Ego-0: Unifying Egocentric Human and Robotic Data for VLA Pretraining¶

    ACE-Ego-0 is a VLA pretraining framework that jointly trains on 4.53K hours of robot/simulation data and 1.48K hours of pseudo-action-labeled egocentric human video. It resolves the representation gap between human and robot data through a unified camera-space action format, cross-embodiment morphology conditioning, and time-aligned action chunking, while handling label noise via a reliability-aware auxiliary loss. The result is state-of-the-art performance on RoboCasa GR1 TableTop and RoboTwin 2.0, plus strong transfer to real bimanual manipulation.

    📌 Why selected: Unifies egocentric human + robot data for scalable VLA pretraining

    μ_0: A Scalable 3D Interaction-Trace World Model¶

    \(\mu_0\) is a 3D trace-space world model that predicts smooth B-spline trajectories for semantic interaction keypoints (objects, tools, hands, contact regions) instead of dense pixels or robot actions. A scalable data engine, TraceExtract, automatically extracts event-captioned 3D trace supervision from heterogeneous human and robot videos. Frozen after video-only pretraining, \(\mu_0\) serves as a reusable motion prior; lightweight action experts trained on top of its features match or exceed action-labeled VLAs like \(\pi_0\).

    📌 Why selected: Scalable 3D interaction-trace world model for embodiment-agnostic robot learning

    APT: Action Expert Pretraining Improves Instruction Generalization of Vision-Language-Action Policies¶

    APT is a two-stage training method for Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models that pretrain the continuous action expert as a language-agnostic Vision-Action (VA) prior before introducing language conditioning. This staged initialization prevents the randomly-initialized action expert from producing noisy gradients that corrupt the VLM backbone, directly addressing why VLA policies fail to generalize to out-of-distribution instructions.

    📌 Why selected: Action expert pretraining improving OOD instruction generalization in VLA models

    World Pilot: Steering Vision-Language-Action Models with World-Action Priors¶

    World Pilot augments Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models with priors from a frozen World-Action Model (WAM) through two complementary injection pathways: Latent Steering, which routes a scene-evolution latent into VLM hidden states via residual cross-attention, and Action Steering, which supplies a trajectory-level prior token to the flow-matching action generator. Together these give the policy anticipatory dynamics knowledge that static image-text pretraining cannot provide, achieving 84.7% on the LIBERO-Plus zero-shot OOD benchmark and top performance across four real-robot manipulation tasks.

    📌 Why selected: World-action priors steering VLA beyond static image-text pretraining

    Embodied-R1.5: Evolving Physical Intelligence via Embodied Foundation Models¶

    Embodied-R1.5 is an 8B-parameter Embodied Foundation Model (EFM) that unifies embodied cognition, task planning, correction, and pointing within a single VLM architecture trained on a 15B-token corpus with a two-stage SFT+RL paradigm. It achieves SOTA on 16 of 24 embodied VLM benchmarks, surpassing Gemini-Robotics-ER-1.5 and GPT-5.4, and can be adapted into a competitive Vision-Language-Action (VLA) model with minimal action data.

    📌 Why selected: R1-style training for embodied VLMs; unified cognition+planning+pointing architecture

    VoLo: A Physical Orchestrator for Open-Vocabulary Long-Horizon Manipulation¶

    VoLo introduces Physical Orchestration, a paradigm where a VLM continuously plans, monitors, and dynamically routes among heterogeneous robot tools (VLA/WAM, perception models, action primitives) in a closed loop—treating the VLA as one interruptible capability rather than a fixed executor. The paper also introduces RoboVoLo, a 126-task benchmark spanning common sense, memory, complex references, and world knowledge for evaluating open-vocabulary long-horizon manipulation.

    📌 Why selected: Stanford/NVIDIA; open-vocab long-horizon manipulation with adaptive planning and recovery

    AffordanceVLA: A Vision-Language-Action Model Empowering Action Generation through Affordance-Aware Understanding¶

    AffordanceVLA introduces structured affordance forecasting as a task-oriented intermediate representation to close the semantic-to-physical-action gap in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models. It disentangles manipulation priors into three parallel sub-tasks—Which2Act, Where2Act, and How2Act—decoded by a Mixture-of-Transformer (MoT) architecture and trained with a three-stage progressive curriculum. On LIBERO, the full model achieves a 95.8% average success rate competitive with the strongest recent VLAs.

    📌 Why selected: Affordance-aware VLA bridging semantic VLM space and embodied action generation

    Robots Need More than VLA and World Models¶

    This position paper argues that scaling Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models alone is insufficient for generalist robotics; the core bottleneck is the absence of grounding mechanisms that convert abundant unstructured physical data (human video, internet video, simulation) into robot-usable supervision. The authors identify four missing components—data interfaces, embodiment interfaces, world-model interfaces, and reward interfaces—and propose a research agenda around a "grounding-centric" pipeline.

    📌 Why selected: Position paper arguing VLA+world models insufficient; frames missing robot intelligence
