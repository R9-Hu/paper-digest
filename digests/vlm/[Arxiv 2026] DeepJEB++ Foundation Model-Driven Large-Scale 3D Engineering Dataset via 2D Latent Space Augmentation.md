# DeepJEB++: Foundation Model-Driven Large-Scale 3D Engineering Dataset via 2D Latent Space Augmentation

*🕒 **Published (v1):** 2026-06-11 07:30 UTC  ·  **Source:** Arxiv  ·  [link](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12994v2)*

## TL;DR
DeepJEB++ is a three-stage pipeline that expands 380 seed jet engine bracket CAD models into 15,360 simulation-labeled 3D meshes (40× expansion) by augmenting in 2D latent space via fine-tuned Stable Diffusion, lifting to 3D with a domain-adapted TRELLIS model, and automatically assigning FEA labels through geometric interface detection. A VLM (LLaVA) serves as the manufacturability gatekeeper for synthesized 2D images. The paper identifies and fixes the "Negative Words Negation" (NWN) artifact, a systematic failure mode in VLM-based quality scoring.

## Problem
3D engineering datasets with physics-based labels are critically scarce: SimJEB offers only 381 hand-designed brackets, and its successor DeepJEB (2,138 samples via DeepSDF) still falls an order of magnitude short of modern architecture requirements. DeepJEB additionally over-smooths geometry by augmenting in an implicit signed-distance field and imposes a single fixed boundary-condition template on every sample, sacrificing both geometric and physical diversity. Extending coverage with web-scale foundation models has previously required large-scale compute and proprietary weights.

## Method
**Stage 1 – 2D latent augmentation.** Stable Diffusion v1.5 is fine-tuned on 7,800 multi-view renders of 300 SimJEB brackets (single A100, 1–2 days). Novel designs are generated by latent interpolation between 1,184 representative pairs selected via PCA + Euclidean distance scoring, sweeping α across 19 steps to yield ~147,000 candidate images. A VLM quality classifier (LLaVA) scores each image by cosine similarity between its text description and 132 LLM-generated negative defect words; the top-p=29% highest-scoring (most defect-like) images are discarded. The NWN fix—adding "Do not use the word 'no'" to the VLM prompt—forces affirmative descriptions (e.g., "the surface is smooth" rather than "no scratches"), preventing negated-defect phrases from spuriously matching the defect vocabulary.

**Stage 2 – image-to-3D lifting.** The fine-tuned TRELLIS model (MIT-licensed; Sparse Structure Generator + SLAT Generator) is domain-adapted on 300 SimJEB brackets using 25 multi-view renders and single-image conditioning. The best-performing single viewpoint—a diagonal three-quarter view—is used for all 22,495 validated designs; this single view matches six-view reconstruction fidelity (Chamfer Distance 0.0031 vs. 0.0032).

**Stage 3 – automated boundary-condition recognition and FEA.** Bolt bores are detected via mesh-adjacency connected components with normal-sweep ≥270° and counterbore filtering. The clevis is found by plane-sectioning and Kåsa-method circle fitting. Detected bolt centers are rigidly registered to the SimJEB template via Umeyama's method. Each mesh is tetrahedralized and solved under four load cases (Ti–6Al–4V, E=113.8 GPa) to produce mass, 95th-percentile von Mises stress, and maximum displacement labels.

## Key Contributions
- 2D-to-3D augmentation strategy exploiting the data-richness asymmetry between 2D image models (billions of training images) and 3D models (hundreds of thousands of shapes)
- Identification and fix of the Negative Words Negation (NWN) problem in VLM-based quality scoring pipelines
- Fully automated boundary-condition recognition pipeline (bolt bore + clevis detection, rigid registration, FEA) requiring no manual intervention
- 40× dataset expansion (380 → 15,360 deployable, simulation-labeled brackets) on single-GPU compute per stage
- Selection of LLaVA over BLIP-2 for fine-grained manufacturing defect detection, motivated by patch-level spatial feature preservation vs. Q-Former compression

## Results
- **Dataset scale:** 380 seed designs → 15,360 deployable simulation-labeled brackets (40× expansion); 9,063 clean single-body + 6,297 additional meshes
- **VLM filter accuracy:** NWN fix raises accuracy on the 636-sample decisive benchmark from 56.45% (vanilla prompt) to 72.17% (default threshold) and 76.10% at p=29% operating point; 76.7% on expanded 908-sample benchmark, confirming generalization
- **TRELLIS fine-tuning (80 held-out test brackets):** IoU: 0.239 → 0.494 (+106.69%); Chamfer Distance: 0.01199 → 0.00731 (−38.98%)
- **Multi-view reconstruction:** Single diagonal view achieves CD=0.0031 vs. six-view CD=0.0032; degenerate views (top/front/bottom) reach CD 0.006–0.016
- **FEA validation vs. SimJEB ground truth (289 brackets):** Median R² = 0.94 (vertical), 0.94 (horizontal), 0.88 (diagonal), 0.91 (torsional); best bracket reaches R²=0.994–0.998 across all load cases
- **Mass label accuracy:** R²=0.9999, median |error|=0.06%, 99% of brackets within 1%

## Limitations
- Demonstrated only on a single engineering domain (jet engine brackets); cross-domain transfer is explicitly left to future work
- VLM filter false-positive rate is 40.7% at the adopted operating point (coarse first-stage screen by design, relying on Stage 3 to enforce geometric validity)
- FEA R² ceiling is below unity due to boundary-condition mismatch (full bolt clamp vs. SimJEB's RBE2 spider permitting rotation) and mesh decimation
- Pipeline yield loss of ~32%: 22,495 generated designs → 15,360 deployable after interface-detection and mesh-quality gates
- NWN fix (prompt negation suppression) is a heuristic that may not generalize to VLMs with different instruction-following behavior

## Relevance to Vision-Language Models
This paper demonstrates a practical, non-semantic application of VLMs as automated fine-grained quality classifiers in an engineering data pipeline, pushing VLM utility beyond high-level scene understanding into manufacturing defect detection. The NWN finding is directly relevant to any pipeline that scores images via cosine similarity between VLM-generated text and a defect/negative vocabulary: negation in natural language systematically inflates defect scores for high-quality samples, and prompt-level suppression of negation words is a simple but meaningful fix. The LLaVA-vs-BLIP-2 comparison provides concrete evidence that patch-level spatial feature preservation (LLaVA's linear projection) outperforms abstractive compression (Q-Former) for localized visual quality assessment, informing architectural choices for VLMs deployed in similar discriminative roles. More broadly, the work illustrates how VLMs can be repurposed as domain-adapted quality gates in multi-stage generative pipelines without task-specific fine-tuning of the VLM itself.
